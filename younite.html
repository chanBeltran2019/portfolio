YouNite

YouNite,

a small app to solve

big social problems.

The Task

YouNite is an imaginary application made for a school assignment. My assignment was to create a social app. I was tasked to make an app icon and include some images of what my app would look like. Lastly, I had to build a mini-website that would market my app. One of my projects, once I graduate college, is to make this project a reality.

The Problem

When brainstorming ideas, I did not want to create just another social app. I wanted to create an app that would solve a problem. During the production of this app, hate towards those of Asian decent was prominent due to the lasting Covid-19 pandemic. People of darker skin are still facing prejudice and given unfair treatment. If only the world was more educated about other cultures, than maybe people can start to understand one another. Then maybe everyone would be treated as equal. By giving everyone a safe place to share their culture, YouNite aims to increase peoples awareness of culture in a fun and social way.

So how does this social app work? With YouNite users will be able to post everything relating to their culture. Through hashtags, users find everyone's post within social topics exampled on the left. There will be multiple sections like Music, Dance, Food, and more. Users can also message other users, create group chats, leave a comment on posts, and explore information on all cultures.

Solution

This platform allows individuals of every culture and ethnicity to have a safe environment to share their culture with others so that people can not only be proud of who they are, but are also educating others in the process. By mimicking all the popular social platforms in the market now in regards to posting, hashtags, commenting, and direct messaging, users will feel right at home with YouNite.

Expected Results

I would expect YouNite to perform well as a platform, but more importantly I expect it to increase the awareness that people have in regards to culture. I firmly believe YouNited has great significance in this society. Now is the time for people to become more aware of each other's differences and more accepting of them. If people were given a safe environment to share and educate others on their culture in a fun and positive way, perhaps individuals would be less likely to create prejudices and stereotypical thoughts about races different to them. In the end, people are afraid of things they do not understand and this would be the perfect platform for individuals to educate themselves.
